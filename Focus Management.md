# Focus Management 

---

## Deep Work

---

### Question 1: What is Deep Work?
- Deep Work is the ability to focus without distraction on cognitively demanding tasks.  
- It involves working in a state of full concentration, producing high‑quality output in less time.  
- It requires eliminating interruptions and entering a flow-like mental state.

---

## Summary of Deep Work Book

---

### Question 2: According to the author, how to do deep work properly?
- Schedule deep work sessions deliberately.  
- Remove distractions aggressively.  
- Work with full intensity, no multitasking.  
- Embrace boredom to train the mind for focus.  
- Have clear goals and specify what “done” looks like.  
- Track time spent in deep work.  
- Prioritize high‑value tasks over shallow tasks.  

---

### Question 3: How can you implement the principles in day‑to‑day life?
- Block specific hours each day for uninterrupted focus.  
- Keep phone and notifications away during work.  
- Use a consistent routine before starting deep work.  
- Reduce time spent on social media and other shallow activities.  
- Set clear daily goals and measurable outcomes.  
- Prepare your workspace to minimize distractions.  
- End each day by planning tomorrow’s deep work window.

---

## Dangers of Social Media

---

### Question 4: What are the dangers of social media, in brief?
- Reduces attention span and increases distraction.  
- Creates addiction through constant dopamine hits.  
- Promotes comparison, anxiety, and low self‑esteem.  
- Consumes time that could be spent on meaningful tasks.  
- Increases stress due to information overload.  
- Weakens ability to focus deeply.
