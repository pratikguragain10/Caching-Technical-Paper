# Listening and Active Listening

---

## 1. What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?

- Understand the meaning of what someone else is saying.
- Don't let your thoughts distract you during the conversation.
- Always focus on the speaker and the topic of conversation.
- Don't interupt while the speaker is talking.
- Always let the speaker finish, then respond with your thoughts about his or her opinion.
- Always show your interest in the conversation.
- Portray that your fully engaged in the conversation through your body language.
- Prefer taking notes during important conversation or meetings.
- Clear you doubts by rephrasing the speaker's statements to ensure that both of you are on the same page or have same common point.

---

## 2. According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?

- As per my understanding the key points of Reflective Listening according to Fisher's model are:

    - Communicating your understanding of speaker's idea back to the speaker in order to confirm that their message has been understood properly.
    - Expressing genuine understanding and empathy in response to a speaker rather than immediately disagreeing, while encouraging them to share more.

---

## 3. What are the obstacles in your listening process?

- My obstacles during my listening process are:

    - Getting distracted by my own thoughts during the conversation.
    - Sometimes showing a lack of interest in the conversation.
    - Not asking questions to clear doubts with the speaker.
    - Not having clarity about the common point or goal of the discussion.

---

## 4. What can you do to improve your listening?

- Be decisive and clear about the topic of discussion.
- Ask questions whenever you have doubts.
- Listen carefully and attentively.
- Stay motivated and show genuine interest in the conversation.
- Socialize more and express yourself confidently.
- Maintain good body posture to show attentiveness and engagement.

## 5. When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day to day life?

- I switch to a passive communication style when I want to avoid arguments or conflicts.
- When someone is angry or speaking loudly, I stay quiet and listen.
- When I am not confident about the topic.
- When the situation doesn’t directly affect me, so I prefer to observe first.

## 7. When do you switch into Passive Aggressive (sarcasm/gossiping/taunts/silent treatment and others) communication styles in your day to day life?

- When I am hurt or disappointed and find it difficult to confront someone.
- When I want to avoid direct conflict but still want to show my feelings.
- When I feel powerless or unable to speak openly.
- When I feel upset but don’t want to express my feelings directly.

--- 

## 8. How can you make your communication assertive? 

- Stay calm and confident while speaking, without raising your voice.
- Listen actively to the other person without interrupting.
- Respect others’ opinions, even if they are different from yours.
- Maintain good body language, like eye contact and a relaxed posture.
- Say “no” politely when necessary, instead of agreeing under pressure.
- Take responsibility for your actions and words instead of blaming others.
- Stay open to feedback and try to solve problems calmly.
